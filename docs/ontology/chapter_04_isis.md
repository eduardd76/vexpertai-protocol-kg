# Chapter 4 IS-IS Ontology

The IS-IS ontology models flooding hierarchy, adjacency, LSP/TLV lineage,
metric style, overload behavior, route leaking, topology modes, and
segment-routing advertisements. Neo4j stores summarized semantic state rather
than the complete link-state database.

## Process, levels, and identity

An `ISISProcess` runs on an `ISISRouter` and has `Level1` and/or `Level2`.
`Level1Level2Router` represents the boundary role. An `ISISArea` contains its
devices. `NET` and `SystemID` remain separate objects so identity and area
membership can be traced without parsing configuration text.

## Adjacency and broadcast behavior

An `ISISAdjacency` forms over an `ISISInterface`. An
`ISISBroadcastSegment` elects a `DIS`, which creates a `Pseudonode`. LSP
lineage can point to either a router or pseudonode.

Repeated DIS changes are represented as summarized segment state and an
`ISISAdjacencyRisk`; raw IIH packets and every election event remain in
telemetry systems.

## LSP and TLV lineage

An `LSP` is generated by a router or pseudonode and contains semantic `ISISTLV`
nodes. A TLV advertises a `Prefix` or `Capability`. This lets route and SID
visibility trace to protocol advertisements without copying all LSP content
into the graph.

## Route leaking

`Level1Route` and `Level2Route` represent important cross-level reachability.
A route can be `LEAKED_BY` a `RouteLeakingPolicy` and `LEAKED_TO` the opposite
level. The policy identifies affected prefixes. Missing relationships are
meaningful: a required Level 2 prefix with no leak to Level 1 explains an
intra-area outage.

## Overload and transit

`OverloadBit` suppresses a `TransitRole`. The router can remain reachable as a
destination while being invalid for transit. This distinction prevents the KG
from treating overload as a simple device-down condition.

## Metrics, topologies, and segment routing

`NarrowMetric` and `WideMetric` specialize `MetricStyle`.
`SingleTopologyISIS`, `MultiTopologyISIS`, and `IPv6Topology` describe address
family topology behavior.

`SegmentRouting` depends on `SegmentRoutingExtension`. `PrefixSID`, `NodeSID`,
and `AdjacencySID` require an advertised capability and point to the IS-IS TLV
that advertises them. A missing advertisement is therefore directly queryable.
